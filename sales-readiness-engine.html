What a seller can do after completing this curriculum

These are the terminal outcomes — the behaviors a rep demonstrates in the field, not the content they consumed in a lesson.

✅ Identify the right use case for a buyer

Given a buyer's title, industry, and discovery signals, the rep names the one or two use cases most likely to resonate — and explains why this customer has this problem.

✅ Ask the questions that surface the need

For each use case, the rep has three to five discovery questions they can deploy naturally — questions that let the customer name their own problem.

✅ Map the problem to Snowflake's capability

The rep can explain specifically how a Snowflake feature solves the customer's stated problem — with current feature names, availability status, and cloud support.

✅ Handle the top objections with confidence

For each use case, the rep knows the three to five objections most likely to come up — and has a response that redirects to Snowflake's differentiated answer.

✅ Reference a proof point from a similar customer

The rep can name a real customer — in the buyer's industry, with the buyer's problem — who solved it with Snowflake. They know the outcome and can reference it in an email the same day.

✅ Practice the conversation before it happens

The rep has run the actual conversation with an AI coach who played the buyer, pushed back with real objections, and scored the response — before they've ever been in front of the customer.

The prototype demonstrates six core capabilities

🎯 Learns who you are, teaches accordingly

A seller selects their role — AE, SE, ACE, or SDR — at the start. Every piece of AI-generated content throughout the session is framed specifically for that role and what they need to know.

📡 Pulls live product data, not static slides

The engine connects directly to Snowflake's Product Launch Tracker (PLT) — the internal database of every Snowflake feature, its release status, target quarter, and supported clouds. When the PLT updates, the engine reflects it. No course author required.

✍️ Writes lesson content on demand

Snowflake's own AI (Cortex) generates a fresh, role-specific lesson overview the moment a seller opens it — grounded in real product data, not a deck written months ago.

🔔 Surfaces what's new automatically

The engine identifies features that launched or updated in the last 90 days and flags them in every lesson. Sellers see what just changed without waiting for a course update cycle.

🤝 Coaches sellers through real conversations

Each lesson includes an AI practice coach with a scenario tailored to the product area and seller role. Reps can rehearse customer conversations before they happen — with immediate AI feedback.

💬 Answers product questions instantly

Sellers can ask free-form questions about any product area — "Which features are GA on Azure?" or "How does this compare to Databricks?" — and get answers grounded in live PLT data, not a search engine.

03 · Curriculum Design

Use Cases Are the Core of Sales Readiness

Knowing a feature is not the same as knowing when a customer needs it. The engine teaches both — and connects them explicitly.

Most enablement stops at product knowledge. A rep finishes a course knowing what Cortex does. They don't know which customer problem to attach it to, which buyer to call, or what question to ask to surface the need. Use case learning closes that gap.

Skill Domains — foundation for Phase 3 reporting

Every lesson is tagged to one of five seller skill domains — the foundation for Phase 3 reporting, so managers see what a rep can do, not just what they completed.

The anatomy of every use case

Every use case taught in the engine follows the same six-part structure. AI assembles this on demand — grounded in PLT features, customer stories from Raven, and sales plays from Seismic.

👤

Buyer Persona + Industry

Who has this problem. What their title is. What industry signals make this a warm conversation. What they read and care about at the executive level.

🔥

The Business Problem

What keeps this buyer up at night. Framed in their language — not Snowflake's. The problem exists before the rep arrives; the rep's job is to name it precisely.

🎯

Snowflake's Solution

The specific product capabilities that solve the problem — pulled live from the PLT. Not a generic pitch. A mapping from their pain to our product, with feature names and release status.

📋

Proof Point

A real customer story — sourced from Raven — where a customer in a similar industry and situation deployed this solution and saw measurable results. Specific is credible.

⚔️

Competitive Angle

Where Databricks, AWS, or the incumbent falls short in this specific use case. Not generic competitive positioning — use-case-specific differentiation that holds up in a technical conversation.

🔁

Objection Map

The three to five objections this buyer raises most often — and the responses that work. Drawn from field patterns, not marketing scripts. The AI coach will use these in the practice session.

03 · Curriculum Design

How Use Cases Are Taught by Role

The same use case is taught four different ways. The AI generates a role-specific frame for each seller — because an AE and an SE need completely different things from the same story.

AE — Account Executive

Business value, access to power, and deal mechanics

The AE use case frame focuses on why the business cares and who to call. For the LLM data exposure use case: what's the financial and reputational risk of a data breach via an external AI API? Who is the economic buyer — the CISO or the CDO? What's the competitive dynamic — is the customer currently evaluating Databricks? What does the business case look like at renewal?

Coach practice: Navigating a CFO who wants to know why Snowflake is worth the premium over "just using OpenAI."

SE — Solutions Engineer

Technical architecture, implementation path, and proof of concept

The SE use case frame goes deep on how it works and how to prove it. For the same use case: what does the Cortex Complete API call look like? How does network policy enforcement prevent data egress? What's the latency profile? How does this integrate with an existing MLOps stack? What does a minimal POC look like that a technical buyer can run in a day?

Coach practice: A skeptical VP of Engineering who says "we already have a fine-tuned model deployed on our own infrastructure."

ACE — Account Cloud Engineer

Expansion, adoption, and customer health

The ACE use case frame focuses on what's already running and where growth comes from. For the same use case: which customers in the book have a data team doing ad hoc ML on Snowflake data today but using external API keys? That's a land-and-expand signal. What does a QBR conversation look like that introduces Cortex Complete to a customer who's been on Snowflake for two years and hasn't touched AI yet?

Coach practice: A long-tenured customer who says "we're happy with what we have — we don't need AI."

SDR — Sales Development Rep

Discovery, qualification, and pipeline creation

The SDR use case frame focuses on ICP signals and the question that opens the door. For the same use case: what job postings, company news, or LinkedIn activity signals that a company is actively building AI and has a CISO who would care about data perimeter? What's the one-sentence problem frame that gets a VP of Data to take a call? How does this use case connect to a specific pain that shows up in the SDR's outbound sequence?

Coach practice: Cold outreach to a Head of Data at a Series C fintech — 30 seconds to earn the meeting.

How use cases integrate into the lesson flow

Use cases are not a separate module — they are woven into every lesson as the connective tissue between product knowledge and practice readiness.

What's live vs. planned: Steps 1 and 6 are fully working in the current prototype. Steps 2–5 are the Phase 2 build — they describe the target lesson design, not what the app does today.

1

Live Now

2

Phase 2

3

Phase 2

Each use case includes three to five discovery questions the rep can use in a real conversation. These are framed for the rep's role — an AE's questions sound different from an SDR's. The questions are designed to let the customer name their own problem.

4

Phase 2

Common objections for each use case are surfaced here — drawn from field patterns. Not generic "why Snowflake" rebuttals. Use-case-specific responses that acknowledge the technical or business concern and redirect to Snowflake's differentiated answer.

5

Phase 2

A real customer story from Raven — matched to the use case by industry and buyer type. The rep sees how a company like their prospect solved the same problem with Snowflake, the measurable outcome, and the quote they can use in an email.

6
AI Practice Coach — run the conversation
Live Now

The coach presents a scenario drawn directly from the use case library — a specific buyer, a specific problem, a specific competitive threat. The rep runs the conversation. The coach plays the buyer, introduces objections from the objection library, and scores the rep's response against use case fluency — not just product knowledge.

How content is governed and validated

The engine does not generate free-form assertions about Snowflake capabilities. Every lesson is grounded in validated source records — the AI's role is synthesis and framing, not invention.

Source grounding

AI-generated content is anchored to explicit PLT records, Raven stories, and Seismic plays. Cortex cannot describe a feature that isn't in the PLT or reference a customer that isn't in Raven. The sources validate; the AI synthesizes.

Feature accuracy

The PLT carries GA status, cloud availability, and preview flags. The engine surfaces these directly — a rep sees "GA on AWS and Azure, Preview on GCP" — and is never shown a claim that contradicts the PLT record.

Enablement review layer

Phase 2 adds a curator dashboard where Enablement can flag, edit, or pin content before it reaches reps. High-stakes lessons — competitive positioning, new launches — can require explicit approval without blocking the rest of the engine.

What this means for the field

A rep who completes a lesson doesn't just know what Snowflake ships — they know which customers have the problem it solves, how to ask the question that surfaces the need, how to handle the top objections, and what customer story to send the next day. That is the difference between product-trained and revenue-ready.

04 · Manager View

What a Manager Sees — and Acts On Phase 3 Roadmap

This section describes planned Phase 3 capabilities. None of the dashboards or reporting features below exist in the current prototype — they are the target state once the analytics layer (USER_EVENTS table + manager app) is built.

Traditional LMS reporting tells a manager who clicked through a course. This engine tells a manager who can actually sell. The difference is behavioral signals — practice activity, topic coverage, coach scores — surfaced at the rep and team level.

What the engine surfaces for managers

🎯

Coverage gaps by product area

Which product areas have zero lesson activity on the team. Which use cases have never been practiced. Where a manager should focus coaching before the next pipeline review.

📊

AI coach engagement by rep

How many practice conversations each rep has run. Which reps are completing lessons without opening the coach — completing the content without doing the work that matters.

🔔

New feature readiness

Which reps have completed lessons on features that launched in the last 90 days. Which reps are likely to mention a feature in a customer call without knowing it's still in preview.

Lesson completion vs. practice completion

The engine distinguishes between a rep who read a lesson and a rep who practiced a scenario. A completion report that conflates the two gives managers a false picture of readiness.

Team-level use case fluency

Across the team, which use cases have the highest coach engagement and the highest objection handling scores. Which use cases are being avoided. What to role-play in the next team meeting.

Individual prep before a big call

A manager can check whether a rep has done the lesson and practice run for the specific use case that matches an upcoming opportunity — before the call, not after the loss.

What managers can do that they can't do today

Before a pipeline review

Pull up the team's readiness dashboard. Identify which reps have gaps in the product area matching the biggest deals in stage. Assign the lesson and the coach scenario. Confirm the rep has practiced before the manager's prep call.

After a product launch

See which reps have completed the new feature lesson within the first two weeks. Follow up with the ones who haven't. Know — before the customer asks — that the team can speak to the new capability accurately.

🔑 Key design principle

The curriculum skeleton (lesson structure, objectives, Bloom's levels) is static code. The content layer (feature lists, summaries, coach responses) is always runtime-generated from live Snowflake data.

🔄 Refresh strategy

Dynamic Tables auto-refresh PLT views every hour. Summary cache table refreshed nightly via Snowflake Task. App reads cache first; falls back to live Cortex Complete on cache miss.

🔐 Auth & access

Deployed as Streamlit in Snowflake — Snowflake SSO handles all auth. Role-based access controls which courses a user can see.

📊 Multi-course ready

One schema, one app framework. Adding a new course = inserting rows into COURSES and LESSONS tables. No code changes required.

DDL — User Progress & Summary Cache
CREATE TABLE ENABLEMENT.SALES_READINESS.USER_EVENTS ( EVENT_ID VARCHAR DEFAULT UUID_STRING(), USER_NAME VARCHAR DEFAULT CURRENT_USER(), COURSE_ID VARCHAR, LESSON_ID INT, EVENT_TYPE VARCHAR, -- 'lesson_view' | 'coach_message' | 'quiz_attempt' EVENT_DATA VARIANT, CREATED_AT TIMESTAMP_NTZ DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP() ); CREATE TABLE ENABLEMENT.SALES_READINESS.SUMMARY_CACHE ( COURSE_ID VARCHAR, LESSON_ID INT, SUMMARY_TEXT TEXT, PLT_SNAPSHOT VARIANT, -- features used to generate this summary GENERATED_AT TIMESTAMP_NTZ DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP(), PRIMARY KEY (COURSE_ID, LESSON_ID) );

Dynamic Table for PLT feature views

Dynamic Table — Auto-refreshing lesson features
CREATE OR REPLACE DYNAMIC TABLE ENABLEMENT.SALES_READINESS.LESSON_FEATURES TARGET_LAG = '1 hour' WAREHOUSE = ENABLEMENT_WH AS SELECT L.COURSE_ID, L.LESSON_ID, L.TITLE AS LESSON_TITLE, P.FEATURE_NAME, P.CURRENT_AVAILABILITY, P.TARGET_QUARTER, P.TARGET_END_DATE, P.AVAILABLE_CLOUDS, P.SEARCH_TEXT, CASE WHEN P.TARGET_END_DATE >= DATEADD(day, -90, CURRENT_DATE()) THEN TRUE ELSE FALSE END AS IS_NEW FROM ENABLEMENT.SALES_READINESS.LESSONS L, SALES.RAVEN.PLT_SEARCH_DATA P, LATERAL FLATTEN(L.PLT_KEYWORDS) KW WHERE P.SEARCH_TEXT ILIKE CONCAT('%', KW.VALUE::VARCHAR, '%') AND P.CURRENT_AVAILABILITY IN ('Generally Available', 'Public Preview', 'Private Preview');
07 · AI Layer

Cortex AI Services

Three Cortex capabilities working together — search, generation, and natural language querying.

🔍

Cortex Search

Semantic vector search over PLT_SEARCH_DATA. Replaces brittle ILIKE keyword matching with meaning-based retrieval. "What features relate to governance?" actually understands the question.

✍️

Cortex Complete

llama3-70b generates lesson summaries and coach responses at runtime. Given PLT features as context, produces accurate, current, sales-ready copy.

💬

Cortex Analyst Phase 2

Today: "Ask the PLT" uses Cortex Complete (llama3-70b) for free-form Q&A — answers are grounded in PLT features surfaced per lesson. Phase 2: Replace with a Cortex Analyst semantic view over PLT_SEARCH_DATA for structured, SQL-backed answers to questions like "Which features are GA on Azure?" — more accurate, auditable, and no hallucination risk.

Cortex Search setup

Create Cortex Search Service
CREATE OR REPLACE CORTEX SEARCH SERVICE ENABLEMENT.SALES_READINESS.PLT_SEARCH ON SEARCH_TEXT ATTRIBUTES FEATURE_NAME, CURRENT_AVAILABILITY, TARGET_QUARTER, AVAILABLE_CLOUDS WAREHOUSE = ENABLEMENT_WH TARGET_LAG = '1 hour' AS SELECT FEATURE_NAME, CURRENT_AVAILABILITY, TARGET_QUARTER, AVAILABLE_CLOUDS, TARGET_END_DATE, SEARCH_TEXT FROM SALES.RAVEN.PLT_SEARCH_DATA WHERE CURRENT_AVAILABILITY IN ('Generally Available', 'Public Preview', 'Private Preview');

Nightly summary refresh (Snowflake Task)

Task — Nightly summary cache refresh
CREATE OR REPLACE TASK ENABLEMENT.SALES_READINESS.REFRESH_SUMMARIES WAREHOUSE = ENABLEMENT_WH SCHEDULE = 'USING CRON 0 2 * * * America/Los_Angeles' AS MERGE INTO ENABLEMENT.SALES_READINESS.SUMMARY_CACHE T USING ( SELECT L.COURSE_ID, L.LESSON_ID, SNOWFLAKE.CORTEX.COMPLETE('llama3-70b', CONCAT('Write a lesson overview for: ', L.TITLE, '. Features: ', LISTAGG(F.FEATURE_NAME, ', ')) ) AS SUMMARY_TEXT FROM ENABLEMENT.SALES_READINESS.LESSONS L JOIN ENABLEMENT.SALES_READINESS.LESSON_FEATURES F USING (COURSE_ID, LESSON_ID) GROUP BY L.COURSE_ID, L.LESSON_ID, L.TITLE ) S ON T.COURSE_ID = S.COURSE_ID AND T.LESSON_ID = S.LESSON_ID WHEN MATCHED THEN UPDATE SET T.SUMMARY_TEXT = S.SUMMARY_TEXT, T.GENERATED_AT = CURRENT_TIMESTAMP() WHEN NOT MATCHED THEN INSERT (COURSE_ID, LESSON_ID, SUMMARY_TEXT) VALUES (S.COURSE_ID, S.LESSON_ID, S.SUMMARY_TEXT);
